Overview

What is EVG Fine Check?

The Emirates Vehicle Gate (EVG) is the UAE's official government-backed digital platform that consolidates traffic fines, vehicle registrations, insurance management, and accident records from all seven emirates in a single portal. Accessible at evg.ae, it connects directly with the Ministry of Interior (MOI), Abu Dhabi Police, RTA Dubai, Sharjah Police, and other traffic authorities.

The EVG fine check service allows UAE vehicle owners to instantly view all outstanding fines using their plate number, Emirates ID, or Traffic Code Number (TCN) from their Mulkiya — no lengthy registration required for basic lookups. More than 2 million traffic fines are recorded across the UAE every year, with speeding accounting for roughly 40% of all violations.

⚠️ Important: Unpaid EVG fines attract a 50% late penalty automatically after the grace period. No warning is sent. Additionally, you cannot renew your Mulkiya (vehicle registration) if any fines remain outstanding.

In 2026, EVG expanded its scope further — Ajman Police users can now access similar functions, making it a truly UAE-wide vehicle services hub. The platform also introduced enhanced UAE Pass integration for faster logins and real-time fine updates.

Step by Step

How to Check EVG Fines — 5 Methods

There are five official ways to check your EVG traffic fines in the UAE. All methods pull from the same government database, so results are identical. Choose whichever is most convenient for you.

🌐 Method 1 — EVG Official Portal (evg.ae)
  1. Visit the official EVG portal at evg.ae
  2. Log in using your UAE Pass or Emirates ID credentials
  3. Enter your plate number, Traffic Code Number (TCN), or Emirates ID
  4. Complete the captcha verification
  5. View all active fines — amounts, violation dates, locations, black points, and photos
  6. Pay securely using credit/debit card, Apple Pay, or bank transfer
  7. Download your PDF receipt or receive SMS confirmation
📱 Method 2 — Abu Dhabi Police App
  1. Download the Abu Dhabi Police App on iOS or Android
  2. Log in with your Emirates ID or UAE Pass
  3. Tap "Traffic Fines" or "EVG Fines"
  4. Enter your vehicle or licence details
  5. View and pay fines directly within the app — works 24/7
🏛️ Method 3 — TAMM Website (Abu Dhabi)
  1. Visit the official TAMM portal (tamm.abudhabi)
  2. Log in or register using UAE Pass
  3. Select "Traffic Fine Payment" under Fines & Violations
  4. Enter your Emirates ID or vehicle plate number when prompted
  5. Authorize any app notification if required
  6. View all outstanding fines linked to your account and pay instantly
🏠 Method 4 — MOI UAE Website
  1. Visit the official MOI UAE website
  2. Select your preferred language
  3. Create a new account or sign in with UAE Pass
  4. Select "Traffic Fines Inquiry" under Traffic Services
  5. Enter your Emirates ID, plate number, or licence number
  6. Click "Search" to view all outstanding fines — pay via card or online banking
📲 Method 5 — SMS (No Internet Required)
  1. Open your SMS app on any UAE mobile number
  2. Type your plate number in the message body
  3. Send to 2220
  4. Receive an instant fine summary via SMS reply — no internet needed
💡 Pro Tip: New violations take 24–72 hours to appear in the EVG system. If you believe you have a fine but it isn't showing, check again after 2 days. Make sure your plate number, emirate, plate color (white/yellow/green/blue), and plate type (private/commercial) match exactly.

Demerit System

UAE Black Points System Explained

The UAE Black Point system, enforced by the Dubai Police and other traffic authorities across the seven emirates, tracks drivers who break traffic laws. Black points are added to your driving licence record each time a traffic violation is recorded. They exist separately from fines — you can pay your fine but the black points remain on your record.

It is important to understand: early payment discounts reduce the fine amount only — they do not remove black points from your driving record. Black points expire automatically after one year from the date of violation.

Know Your Rights

How to Dispute an EVG Traffic Fine

If you believe a traffic fine was issued in error — for example, if your vehicle was captured by a radar you were not driving, or if there is a technical error in the record — you have the right to formally dispute it. Disputes do not guarantee cancellation, but may result in a reduction of the fine or a waiver of black points.

  1. Log into the EVG portal at evg.ae and locate the specific fine you wish to dispute.
  2. Click "Submit Objection" on the fine's detail page — this is the fastest route for most violations.
  3. Gather supporting evidence: video footage, photographs, witness statements, or any documentation relevant to the case.
  4. For Abu Dhabi violations, you can also submit a formal dispute through the TAMM portal or the Abu Dhabi Police website directly.
  5. Keep copies of all documents submitted. Follow up on your complaint reference number for resolution status.
  6. Alternatively, call EVG customer support at 600 588 888 or email contact@evg.ae for assistance.
⚠️ Do not pay a fine you plan to dispute. Payment is generally treated as acceptance of the violation. Only dispute fines where you have legitimate grounds — false disputes waste time and may be flagged.
Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

What is EVG and how does it work? ›
The Emirates Vehicle Gate (EVG) is the UAE's official online platform for vehicle-related government services. It connects with Abu Dhabi Police, RTA Dubai, MOI, Sharjah Police, and other traffic authorities to give you a unified view of all your traffic fines, vehicle registrations, insurance records, and accident history across all seven emirates. You access it via evg.ae using your UAE Pass or Emirates ID.
How long does it take for a fine to appear in EVG? ›
New violations typically take 24 to 72 hours to appear in the EVG system after being recorded by a radar camera or traffic officer. If you believe you received a fine but it is not showing, wait 2–3 days and check again. Make sure you are entering the correct plate number, emirate, plate color, and plate type (private/commercial).
Can I renew my Mulkiya with outstanding fines? ›
No. You must clear all outstanding traffic fines before renewing your vehicle registration (Mulkiya). Driving with an expired Mulkiya results in an AED 500 fine plus 4 black points and possible vehicle impoundment. Always perform an EVG fine check before your registration renewal date.
Do early payment discounts remove black points? ›
No. Early payment discounts reduce the fine amount only. Black points remain on your driving record for one year from the date of the violation and expire automatically. Special campaigns around UAE National Day sometimes offer black point cancellations as a separate component — but these are announced officially by each emirate's police and are not a standard part of the regular discount system.
What happens if I reach 24 black points? ›
Your driving licence is automatically suspended and your vehicle may be impounded. You will be required to surrender your licence to the relevant traffic authority. In some cases, mandatory driving re-testing or rehabilitation courses are required before your licence is reinstated. Regularly check your black point balance via EVG to avoid reaching this threshold.
Can rental cars get EVG fines? ›
Yes. If you were driving a rental car during a violation, the fine is initially issued to the registered owner (the rental company). Rental companies in the UAE typically transfer fines to the driver who rented the vehicle at the time, often with an additional admin fee. They may handle payment directly and charge your card on file. Always check your EVG record after returning a rental.
Is there VAT on EVG traffic fines? ›
No. UAE traffic fines do not attract 5% VAT. The amount shown in your EVG fine check is the amount you pay — there are no hidden tax additions on top of the fine itself.
How do I check if a used car has accident history before buying? ›
Use the EVG accident check feature available at evg.ae. Log in with UAE Pass, navigate to "Traffic Accidents Management," and enter the vehicle's chassis number (VIN). This pulls official government records and shows any past incidents, accidents, or damage reports registered by UAE traffic and police authorities. This is highly recommended before any used car purchase in the UAE.
What payment methods does EVG accept? ›
EVG accepts Visa and Mastercard credit/debit cards, Apple Pay, and direct bank transfers. For large fines, instalment plans are available through partner banks including ADCB, Emirates NBD, FAB, and Mashreq — typically interest-free when paid via credit card, subject to a minimum fine amount and bank approval.
What is the EVG customer support contact? ›
You can reach EVG customer support by calling 600 588 888 (UAE toll rate), emailing contact@evg.ae, or using the "Contact Us" section on the official evg.ae website. Support is available during UAE government working hours.
